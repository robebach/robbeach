You may fit the perfect profile of a bar owner. 
You could call it Bar and Whiskers and have a nerd and bicycle theme.
You will then surely meet many hot women (and artists for that matter).

I, personally, will never own a food or beverage store because I think it is not worth being tide down permanently. Now don't get me wrong, kids do not mean tide down in this way. Kids have legs and brains. Resteraunts have employees and, worse, customers (much worse). However, Bar and Whiskers is a name that I would frequent because due to occupational hazards have an unhealthy taste for beer, data and art. I jump around from project to project, in and out of personal then work life. Head can jump around internally while the facade reveals nothing (or so I think) methinks sometimes the olde wifey knows a thing or two and given time all infractions will be laid bare as my ass last night.

Bar and whiskers also has intonations of hipster flavor known as the mustach. All the varieties as displayed in the mustach book at "Mister" down Butler. The data vibe is inspired by the surrounding and nearby mini-silicone, more like robotics central. Google and CMU, the Chinese must be involved. Yinzers' still steel through and through, but have abandoned the city, left as scraps for the hipsters to fill in between the other neglected communities. 

We have been pushed, and shoveled; buldozed down the way to new locations our entire existance. Stability is only relative to very short periods of times in our insignificant lives. They are, our lives that is, the only point of reference we have to go by. Seemingly a point within the larger cosmos looking out. Inferring even beyond our direct shape looking beyond the detectible for milenia. Don't be misled by a measely 1000, at most scale (seemingly) that scale is not so big. The distribution seems to be scewed toward the small, because no doubt the most complext of structures need not be large to be breathtaking. The vasness is irrelevent in some sense due to the complexity right under our noses. Big is cool, but small may be the key.
