We are thinking the selection ideally would be based on heat-loss/gain calcs (manJ) potentially including solar loads. Basic internal airflows such as a stack-effect ventilation calculation to allocate load may be used to identify sets of dampers that are set up at commissioning to behave according to their “type”. Im not even sure it would increase the duct count more than current practice for cold-climate designs which have to design to both heating and cooling loads. These automated dampers allow a shifting of air among the rooms of the homes without increasing static pressure. I would like to explore a system with zero communications between the dampers except their pre-programmed response to local sensors.
