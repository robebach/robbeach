Client has some data
or not
That data tells us very specific things
unless it's bad
But nonetheless there is a building or community
Those buildings consume energy
That consumption is dependent on a lot of things
such as the occupant and the weather
These factors have significant variation
leading to changes in energy use
Modeling can be used to flush out what is causing that auspicious number to go up or down
We make assumptions - the building america simulation protocol is a good place to start because it is based on measurements and represents an average
No one is average unless they are really boring.
No one will admit to being boring so!
deviations from assumptions tell us what the building is and is not.
sort of
this is where it starts to get really fuzzy.
We can use models based on physics to determine relationships that are reasonable and even impossible.

At the community scale what was difficult just got multiplied.
Its a fair democracy so every building gets the same tender loving care but there is no time.
